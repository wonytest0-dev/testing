PARK JIMIN CAREER

Explore Jimin’s career journey, achievements, and awards from 2018 to present. This timeline highlights his global impact, chart success, and influence as a solo artist and member of BTS.

Pre-debut

Park Jimin was born on October 13, 1995, in Busan, South Korea, where he developed an early interest in performance, particularly dance. During middle school, he trained at Just Dance Academy, where he learned urban dance styles such as popping and locking.

He later entered Busan High School of Arts, where he specialized in contemporary dance and was recognized as one of the top students in the modern dance department. A teacher encouraged him to audition for Big Hit Entertainment, after which he moved to Seoul to become a trainee and transferred to Korean Arts High School.

This combination of hip-hop foundations and formal contemporary training would later become a defining element of his performance identity, distinguishing him from many idols trained primarily in one system.

2013–2022: Career as a Member of BTS

Jimin debuted in 2013 as a member of BTS under Big Hit Entertainment, taking on the roles of main dancer and vocalist. In the group’s early years, he was noted for his strong technical foundation, particularly his control, body lines, and expressive movement quality.

As BTS expanded globally, Jimin’s role evolved beyond performance execution into interpretive artistry. His solo performances within BTS albums, including “Lie” (2016), “Serendipity” (2017), and “Filter” (2020), demonstrated a continued exploration of narrative and character through performance.

During this period, he also developed a significant public presence, consistently ranking highly in Korean brand reputation rankings and reflecting both domestic popularity and global recognition.

2018–2022: Early Individual Work and Artistic Direction

While continuing group activities, Jimin began to establish an individual artistic voice. In 2018, he released “Promise,” a self-produced track distributed online, marking an early step toward personal storytelling.

In 2022, he collaborated with Ha Sung-woon on “With You” for the drama Our Blues. The track debuted at number one on the Billboard Digital Song Sales chart and reached the top five of the Billboard Global 200.

2023–2024: Solo Debut and Expansion of Artistic Identity

Jimin formally debuted as a solo artist with FACE in March 2023. Its lead single, “Like Crazy,” debuted at number one on the Billboard Hot 100, marking a historic milestone for a Korean solo artist.

In 2024, he released MUSE, continuing to explore personal themes. The single “Who” reached number one on the Billboard Global 200, further solidifying his global impact.

2023–2025: Military Service

Jimin enlisted in the Republic of Korea Army on December 12, 2023. During training, he received multiple commendations and ranked first among trainees. He was discharged on June 11, 2025.

Post-Military Career (2025–Present)

Following his discharge, Jimin resumed activities as both a member of BTS and a solo artist, maintaining strong global presence across music platforms and charts.

His continued chart performance and constant global attention reflect a sustained influence beyond typical K-pop career cycles, bridging idol success with long-term artistic development.
