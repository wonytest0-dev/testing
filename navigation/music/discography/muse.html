Jimin's Solo Album MUSE

Released in July 2024, MUSE is Jimin’s second solo album, exploring themes of inspiration, love, and longing. The project reflects the emotional journey of searching for a muse—someone who awakens creativity, passion, and deeper feelings.

MUSE is a solo album by Jimin showcasing his artistic growth, musical versatility, and global influence. The album features multiple tracks that achieved strong chart performance worldwide.

With the title track Who, the album highlights Jimin’s expressive vocals and modern pop sound, continuing his artistic evolution after FACE.

SERENADE ver. (Yellow): Featuring a retro band-style aesthetic with a yellow color palette, symbolizing the process of conveying feelings.

Jimin's second solo album MUSE (released July 19, 2024) has been a massive success, building on his debut FACE with strong chart longevity, sales, streaming milestones, and record-breaking feats—especially on global platforms like Billboard. As of mid-March 2026 (current date March 15, 2026), it continues to chart impressively nearly 20 months after release.

ACHIEVEMENT:

  • Billboard World Albums Chart: The longest-charting K-pop solo album in history, with 85 consecutive weeks on the chart (as of early March 2026, ranking #23 that week). It's the first and only K-pop solo album to achieve this longevity. Jimin topped the chart 8 times with MUSE (adding to 5 from FACE for a total of 13 #1 weeks as a soloist). It ranked #11 on the 2024 year-end World Albums chart and #3 on the 2025 year-end, making Jimin the only artist with three consecutive years in the top ranks (including FACE at #5 in 2023).
  • Billboard 200: Debuted at #2 (tied as the highest for a K-pop soloist), with 96,000 equivalent album units in its first week (including 74,000 pure sales). It spent multiple weeks in the chart, including re-entries and surges (e.g., massive vinyl sales boost in late 2025).
  • Longest Charting Album Chart on Billboard 200 (34 weeks)
  • South Korea (Circle Chart): Certified 2x Platinum (over 500,000 certified copies; actual sales exceeded 938,000+ copies tracked).
  • First-week sales: Over 771,000 copies in South Korea; Hanteo first-week around 814,000.
  • Japan: Gold certification (RIAJ); debuted #3 on Oricon Albums.
  • Global: Strong pure sales including vinyl resurgence in the US.
  • Top 10 in multiple countries at debut (France, Portugal, Austria, Switzerland, Germany).
  • iTunes: Album topped charts in 115 countries/regions.
  • Over 3.7 billion streams on Spotify.
  • Lead single "Who" surpassed 1.2 billion streams.
  • Album contributed to Jimin becoming one of the most-streamed K-pop soloists.
  • Longest time in Top 10 on Billboard World Albums by an Asian soloist.
  • Helped secure Jimin as a top global artist.

MUSE (featuring upbeat tracks like "Who" and the pre-release "Smeraldo Garden Marching Band") showcased Jimin's versatility and global appeal, earning widespread praise for its production and emotional depth.

WHO

▶
▶

Jimin's "Who" (lead single from his second solo album MUSE, released July 19, 2024) is one of the most successful K-pop solo tracks in history, blending pop, R&B, and dance elements with Jimin's signature vocals and charismatic performance. It broke numerous records upon release and maintained massive longevity, especially on streaming platforms and charts.

ACHIEVEMENT:

  • Surpassed 2.3 billion streams (as of February 19, 2026), achieved in 581 days—making it the first and only song by an Asian native soloist to reach this milestone.
  • Fastest K-pop solo song to 1 billion streams (under 118 days), dominating global daily charts.
  • Contributed to Jimin's solo streaming dominance with over 6.5 billion streams from pure solo tracks.
  • Billboard Hot 100: Debuted at #14, peaked at #12, and spent 33 non-consecutive weeks on the chart.
  • Global 200 & Global Excl. U.S.: Debuted at #1 on both charts.
  • Extended chart longevity into 2026 with 83+ weeks on Global Excl. U.S.
  • RIAA (U.S.): Eligible for 5× Platinum (over 5 million units).
  • Music Canada: Double Platinum (160,000+ units).
  • Topped iTunes charts worldwide and earned multiple music show wins in Korea.
  • Music video surpassed 100M+ YouTube views by 2025.
  • Helped establish Jimin as one of the most streamed K-pop soloists globally.
  • First solo song by a Korean/K-pop artist this decade and the first male K-pop soloist to be BRIT Certified GOLD.

"Who" solidified Jimin's global solo superstar status, earning widespread acclaim from outlets like Billboard for its crossover appeal and enduring popularity.

Be Mine

▶

Jimin's "Be Mine" is a standout track from his second solo album MUSE (released July 19, 2024). It's an upbeat, sensual pop song with Spanish guitar influences, catchy hooks, and themes of longing and desire ("I want you to be mine"). An English version was also released as a single/visualizer in August 2024, boosting its global appeal. While not the lead single (that's "Who"), "Be Mine" emerged as a strong fan-favorite album cut with impressive longevity, viral moments, and streaming success—especially notable for a non-title track.

ACHIEVEMENT:

  • Surpassed 365 million streams globally (per recent Spotify data; earlier milestones included 300M in October 2025 after 444 days, 325M+ by November 2025, and 350M by January 2026).
  • Fastest non-single song by an Asian soloist to reach 250 million streams.
  • Became Jimin's seventh (later more) solo track to surpass 300 million streams.
  • Recorded strong daily surges, including over 816,000 streams in a single day in 2025.
  • Re-entered charts multiple times (e.g., US Spotify #160 in October 2025 with 324,000+ streams in a week).
  • Peaked at No. 89 on the Billboard Global Excl. U.S. chart.
  • Maintained long-term presence on Spotify US and Global charts.
  • English version topped iTunes charts in multiple markets (e.g., #1 in Mexico).
  • Contributed to Jimin's overall chart dominance and cumulative nationwide chart records.
  • Helped solidify MUSE as a massive streaming success.
  • Praised for its production, sensual vibe, and Jimin's vocal versatility.
  • Often compared to Jimin's earlier hit "Filter."
  • Boosted overall album streams, sales, and fan engagement.

"Be Mine" exemplifies Jimin's ability to turn album tracks into long-lasting hits through consistent streaming power and fan support, adding to his status as a global streaming powerhouse even for non-lead singles.

Closer Than This

▶

Jimin's "Closer Than This" is a heartfelt, emotional track from his second solo album MUSE (released July 19, 2024), but it was first unveiled as a digital single on December 22, 2023 (during his military service era). It's an uplifting pop ballad with warm production, themes of closeness and gratitude to Fans, and Jimin's signature tender vocals. It served as a special gift to fans and became a strong streaming performer despite not being a full promo single like "Who" or pre-releases.

ACHIEVEMENT:

  • Surpassed 321 million streams globally.
  • Hit 300 million streams by September 2025, 310 million by December 2025, and 320 million by early March 2026.
  • One of the standout non-lead tracks from MUSE, contributing to the album's massive streaming success (over 3.8 billion Spotify streams by early 2026).
  • Helped Jimin reach broader milestones: over 8 billion total Spotify streams and 6.6+ billion pure solo streams.
  • Spotify Global: Debuted at #36 with over 3 million streams in its first tracking week.
  • Billboard charts at release: #1 World Digital Song Sales, #1 Digital Song Sales, and strong global chart entries.
  • Maintained steady streaming growth into 2026 with continued chart rebounds.
  • Became a fan-favorite “gift song” during Jimin's enlistment era.

"Closer Than This" stands out as a quiet but enduring hit, proving Jimin's emotional ballads hold massive staying power even without heavy promotion—adding to his legacy as one of the top-streamed K-pop soloists ever.

MUSE VINYL

Jimin's second solo album MUSE (released in 2024) has a vinyl edition that's been a big hit among fans, especially with its physical release and pre-orders starting around August 2025, followed by availability in late 2025 (e.g., rolling releases, with some color variants like burgundy or turquoise mentioned in fan discussions).

ACHIEVEMENT:

  • The vinyl release boosted sales significantly. For example, in the U.S., after the vinyl drop, MUSE saw a massive surge (over 5,000% week-over-week in some reports), debuting inside the Top 10 on Billboard's Vinyl Albums Chart (e.g., #8 in one tracking week around November 2025).
  • This contributed to broader milestones for the album, like surpassing 500,000 units sold in the U.S. (pure sales + equivalents) by early 2025, and global equivalent album sales exceeding 6 million units.

The vinyl edition has been part of ongoing fan campaigns and "MUSE Vinyl era" excitement on social media, with many Fans pre-ordering or collecting it as a key physical item. It's still in stock at various retailers as of March 2026.
